# CFRA & Pregnancy Disability Leave — Required Workplace Poster (English)

## What this file is

Form number: CRD-100-21ENG, January 2025. English. 1 page. Color poster with graphic design. Employers with 5+ employees must post this in a conspicuous location.

## What it's for / when it's used
Required workplace posting under CFRA and California PDL law. Employers with 5+ employees must display this poster. It summarizes employee rights at a high level; it is not a certification form. Workers' comp clients whose employers failed to post this may have an argument for non-disclosure of their rights — relevant to whether notice requirements were met.

## Key information an LLM needs
**CFRA leave entitlement (from poster):**
- Up to 12 work weeks per 12-month period
- Eligibility: 12+ months service, 1,250+ hours in preceding 12 months, employer with 5+ employees
- Covered reasons: employee's own serious health condition; serious health condition of covered family member (child, spouse, domestic partner, parent, parent-in-law, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, "designated person," or anyone with family-like relationship); birth/adoption/foster care bonding
- Intermittent leave available when medically necessary
- Pay: law guarantees unpaid leave only; some employers may pay; EDD Paid Family Leave also available

**Pregnancy Disability Leave (separate from CFRA bonding leave):**
- Up to 4 months when disabled by pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical condition
- Available regardless of employer size (as long as 5+ employees) and regardless of CFRA eligibility
- Can take BOTH PDL and CFRA: PDL for pregnancy disability, then CFRA for baby bonding — PDL does not count against CFRA 12-week entitlement
- Reinstatement to same position (or comparable) guaranteed

**Notice requirements:**
- 30 days advance notice for foreseeable events (planned surgery, scheduled birth)
- As soon as practicable for unforeseeable events (at minimum, verbal notice)
- Failing to provide notice may result in deferral of leave

**Certification:** Employers may require HCP certification for serious health condition (see `cfra-certification-en`)

## Open questions
- January 2025 revision is current as of 2026-06-18 — this is relatively recent. Check calcivilrights.ca.gov/posters/required to confirm no newer version has been issued.
- Employers with fewer than 5 employees are not covered by CFRA or PDL — this poster's content would not apply to those clients' employers. However, they may still be covered by FMLA (50+ employees within 75 miles) or other protections.
