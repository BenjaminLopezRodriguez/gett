# CFRA Certification of Health Care Provider (English)

## What this file is
California Family Rights Act (CFRA) medical certification form completed by a licensed health care provider to support an employee's request for family/medical leave. Issued by California Civil Rights Department (CRD). Form number: CRD-E11P-ENG, January 2023. English. 3 pages. This form also doubles as a dual CFRA/FMLA certification where both laws apply.

## What it's for / when it's used
Employees in California (at employers with 5+ employees) request CFRA leave for their own serious health condition, to care for a family member, or for military exigency. The employer gives the employee this form to hand to their treating physician. The physician completes items 1–9 (and item 10 when applicable). The employee completes item 10 separately under separate cover when claiming leave to care for a family member with a serious health condition.

Employer uses the completed form to determine CFRA leave eligibility and duration. Employers may not ask for more medical information than what the form requests.

## Key information an LLM needs
**CalGENICA warning (top of form):** The Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act prohibits employers from requesting genetic information. The form explicitly instructs the HCP: "Do NOT provide genetic information, including family medical history." This must be flagged to any physician completing the form.

**Critical CFRA/FMLA distinction:** Pregnancy is a serious health condition under FMLA but is **NOT** a serious health condition under CFRA. When leave is for pregnancy disability, the employee uses California Pregnancy Disability Leave (PDL) under CFRA rather than the standard CFRA serious health condition category.

**Form items (physician completes 1–9):**
1. Patient's name and medical facts about the condition (diagnosis optional — physician may describe symptoms/functional limitations)
2. Approximate date condition commenced and probable duration
3. Is hospitalization required? (Yes/No)
4. Is employee/family member unable to perform any of their job functions? Which functions? Duration?
5. Is employee needed to care for family member? Duration?
6. Will employee/family member need intermittent leave or a reduced schedule? Frequency/duration of episodes?
7. Is condition a chronic/continuing condition? (Flare-up or episodic?)
8. Is the condition permanent or long-term?
9. Is the condition that of a covered family member?

**Item 10** (filled by employee separately, not physician): Statement that employee's presence is needed to care for family member. Must be submitted under separate cover — not shown to HCP.

**Signatures required:** Health Care Provider (with license number, type, address, phone) AND employee signature/date.

**Employer turnaround:** Employer must notify employee of approval/denial within 5 business days of receiving certification (or within 2 business days of receiving sufficient information to make determination).

**Intermittent leave:** Form specifically addresses frequency and duration of episodic absences — critical for workers' comp clients who need ongoing medical appointments.

## Open questions
- January 2023 revision. CRD periodically updates CFRA forms; check calcivilrights.ca.gov for newer version before production use.
- Some employers use their own CFRA certification forms — legally permissible if they request no more information than this form. This form is the safe baseline.
