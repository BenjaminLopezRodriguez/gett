# DWC Form RFA — Request for Authorization for Medical Treatment

## What this file is
California workers' compensation form used by the primary treating physician to formally request authorization for specific medical treatment from the claims administrator. Initiates the Utilization Review (UR) process required by Labor Code §4610. Issued by California Division of Workers' Compensation (DWC). Form number: DWC Form RFA, version 01/2014. English. 2 pages (1 form + 1 instruction page).

## What it's for / when it's used
Filed by the requesting physician when they need authorization for treatment that goes beyond what is already approved. Required for the UR process under Labor Code §4610. If the claims administrator denies or modifies the request, the physician or employee can appeal through Independent Medical Review (IMR).

Must be accompanied by supporting documentation: Doctor's First Report (Form DLSR 5021), a Treating Physician's Progress Report (DWC Form PR-2), or equivalent narrative report substantiating the requested treatment.

**Privacy warning on the form:** "Private healthcare information is contained in the Request for Authorization for Medical Treatment, DWC Form RFA. The form can only go to other treating providers and to the claims administrator."

## Key information an LLM needs
**Type of request (checkboxes):**
- New Request
- Resubmission – Change in Material Facts
- Expedited Review (imminent and serious threat to employee's health — requires documentation)
- Written confirmation of prior oral request

**Employee Information:** Name (Last, First, Middle), Date of Injury, Date of Birth, Claim Number, Employer.

**Requesting Physician Information:** Name, Practice Name, Contact Name, Address, City, State, Zip, Phone, Fax, Specialty, NPI Number, Email.

**Claims Administrator Information:** Company Name, Contact Name, Address, City, State, Zip, Phone, Fax, Email.

**Requested Treatment table (up to 5 rows):**
- Diagnosis (Required)
- ICD-Code (Required)
- Service/Good Requested (Required)
- CPT/HCPCS Code (if known)
- Other Info: Frequency, Duration, Quantity, etc.

**Requesting Physician Signature and Date** (mandatory).

**Claims Administrator/URO Response section** (lower portion, completed by claims admin):
- Approved / Denied or Modified (see separate decision letter) / Delay (see notification) / Previously denied / Liability disputed
- Authorization Number (if assigned), Date, Authorized Agent Name, Signature, Phone, Fax, Email, Comments

**Routing:** Can be mailed, faxed, or e-mailed to the address designated by the claims administrator.

**Response deadlines:** Claims administrator must respond within timeframes in Labor Code §4610 and 8 CCR §9792.9.1. For Expedited Review: response required within 72 hours of receipt.

**Not separately reimbursable:** DWC Form RFA is not a separately reimbursable report under the Official Medical Fee Schedule (8 CCR §9789.10 et seq.).

**MTUS note:** For treatments inconsistent with the Medical Treatment Utilization Schedule (MTUS, 8 CCR §9792.20 et seq.) or for conditions not addressed by MTUS, physician may include peer-reviewed scientific evidence to justify the request.
