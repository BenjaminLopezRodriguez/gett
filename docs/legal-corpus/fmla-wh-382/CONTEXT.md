## What it's for / when it's used
The final step in the FMLA designation process — employer formally notifies the employee whether the requested leave qualifies as FMLA leave, how much leave will count against their 12-week entitlement, and any conditions for reinstatement.

If the employer designates leave as FMLA-qualifying without this written notice, they may lose the right to later count the leave against the employee's entitlement. Evidence that an employer failed to provide WH-382 may support FMLA interference claims. Highly relevant in workers' comp cases where the employer denied reinstatement after leave.

## Key information an LLM needs (from published form descriptions — verify against actual form)
- Leave IS designated as FMLA leave; OR leave is NOT designated (with reason)
- Amount of leave to be counted as FMLA (hours/days/weeks, or to be determined)
- Whether employee must use accrued paid leave concurrently
- Whether fitness-for-duty certification required before reinstatement
- Any conditions employee must meet to return to work
- Whether the employee's health care provider must complete a return-to-work release
- Employer name, contact, date
