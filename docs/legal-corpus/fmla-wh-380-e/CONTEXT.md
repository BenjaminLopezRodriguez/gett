## What it's for / when it's used
Used when an employee requests FMLA leave for their own serious health condition (as opposed to WH-380-F which is for a family member's condition). The employer provides this form to the employee, who takes it to their treating physician. The physician must return the completed form within 15 calendar days. Employer uses it to determine FMLA leave eligibility and duration.

FMLA applies to employers with 50+ employees within 75 miles of the worksite; employees must have 12+ months employment and 1,250+ hours in the past 12 months.

**Contrast with California:** In California, CFRA (similar to FMLA) applies to employers with 5+ employees. The California equivalent is `cfra-certification-en`. If both FMLA and CFRA apply, a single certification can satisfy both — many courts accept one form for both laws.

## Key information an LLM needs (from published form descriptions — verify against actual form)
- Patient name, date of birth, date condition commenced, probable duration
- Description of the appropriate medical facts about the condition (diagnosis need not be disclosed — functional limitations sufficient)
- Whether patient can perform any work at all; whether patient can perform essential functions of their specific position
- Whether intermittent or reduced-schedule leave is medically necessary (frequency and duration of episodes)
- Whether condition is a chronic/continuing condition or single event
- HCP signature, license number, specialty, address, phone
- Employee signature certifying authorization for disclosure

**FMLA-specific note:** Pregnancy is a serious health condition under FMLA (unlike CFRA where PDL is separate). Use WH-380-E for employee's own pregnancy-related serious health condition leave.
