## What it's for / when it's used
Employer must provide this notice to the employee within 5 business days of learning that the employee may need FMLA leave. It tells the employee whether they are eligible for FMLA and, if not, why not. It also describes the employee's rights and responsibilities during FMLA leave.

Evidence that an employer failed to provide WH-381 may support a claim of FMLA interference or retaliation. Relevant to workers' comp cases where the employer failed to notify the employee of their FMLA rights.

## Key information an LLM needs (from published form descriptions — verify against actual form)
- Employer notifies employee whether they ARE or ARE NOT eligible (with specific reason if ineligible)
- Eligibility reasons for denial: less than 12 months employment, less than 1,250 hours worked in past 12 months, worksite has fewer than 50 employees within 75 miles
- Employer designates what leave will count as FMLA leave
- Instructions for providing HCP certification (which form, deadline for return — 15 calendar days)
- Whether employer will require fitness-for-duty certification on return
- Whether employee must use accrued paid leave concurrently with FMLA leave
- Employer's name, contact, employee name, date
