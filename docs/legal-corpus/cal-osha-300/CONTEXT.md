# Cal/OSHA Form 300 — Log of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses

## What this file is
California-specific OSHA injury and illness log, required for employers covered by Cal/OSHA. Issued by California Division of Occupational Safety and Health (Cal/OSHA), Department of Industrial Relations. Form number: Cal/OSHA Form 300, Rev. 7/2007, Appendix A. English. 1-page log form (employers print as many copies as needed). Authority: California Code of Regulations (CCR) Title 8 §14300.29(b)(6)-(10). Recording criteria per CCR Title 8 §14300.8 through §14300.12.

## What it's for / when it's used
Maintained by California employers on an ongoing basis throughout the year, one log per establishment. Records every work-related death and every work-related injury or illness that involves loss of consciousness, restricted work activity or job transfer, days away from work, or medical treatment beyond first aid. Also records significant work-related injuries/illnesses diagnosed by a physician or licensed HCP that meet specific recording criteria in CCR Title 8 §14300.8–14300.12.

Distinct from workers' comp (DWC Form 1, Form 5021) — this is an employer safety recordkeeping obligation under Cal/OSHA, separate from the injured worker's WC claim.

## Key information an LLM needs
**Column structure:**
- **(A)** Case number
- **(B)** Employee's name
- **(C)** Job title (e.g., Welder)
- **(D)** Date of injury or onset of illness (month/day)
- **(E)** Where the event occurred (e.g., Loading dock north end)
- **(F)** Describe injury or illness, parts of body affected, and object/substance that directly injured or made person ill (e.g., "Second degree burns on right forearm from acetylene torch")

**Classify the case — most serious outcome (check ONLY one):**
- **(G)** Death
- **(H)** Days away from work
- **(I)** Remained at work — Job transfer or restriction
- **(J)** Other recordable cases

**Number of days:**
- **(K)** Days the injured/ill worker was away from work
- **(L)** Days on job transfer or restriction

**Illness type (column M — check one):**
1. Injury
2. Skin disorder
3. Respiratory condition
4. Poisoning
5. Hearing loss
6. All other illnesses

**Confidentiality notice (printed on form):** "This form contains information relating to employee health and must be used in a manner that protects the confidentiality of employees to the extent possible while the information is being used for occupational safety and health purposes."

**Important:** Page totals must be transferred to Form 300A (Summary) before posting.

**Companion form:** For each case recorded on Form 300, employer must also complete a Cal/OSHA Form 301 (Injury and Illness Incident Report) or equivalent within 7 calendar days.

**Employee access rights:** Employees, former employees, and representatives have the right to review the Form 300 in its entirety (CCR Title 8 §14300.35). Limited access to Form 301 per CCR Title 8 §14300.35.

## Open questions
- Rev. 7/2007 — nearly 20 years old. Check dir.ca.gov/dosh/etools/recordkeeping/ for updates. Cal/OSHA recordkeeping requirements were updated in 2017 (electronic submission added); verify form is still current.
- Cal/OSHA recordkeeping exemptions differ slightly from federal OSHA — fewer industries are exempt in California. Confirm applicable exemptions for client's employer industry.
