# Cal/OSHA Form 301 — Injury and Illness Incident Report

## What this file is
California incident-level detail form, completed for each recordable work-related injury or illness logged on the Cal/OSHA Form 300. Issued by California Division of Occupational Safety and Health (Cal/OSHA), Department of Industrial Relations. Form number: Cal/OSHA Form 301, Appendix C. English. 1-page form. Authority: CCR Title 8 §14300.33 (5-year retention); CCR Title 8 §14300.29(b)(6)-(10) (confidentiality).

## What it's for / when it's used
One Form 301 is completed for each case recorded on the Form 300 Log. Must be completed within 7 calendar days of receiving information that a recordable work-related injury or illness has occurred. Kept for 5 years per CCR Title 8 §14300.33.

State workers' compensation insurance, OSHA reports, or other equivalent reports may be acceptable substitutes, as long as they contain all the same information requested on this form.

Employees have limited access to Form 301 (full access to Form 300 per CCR Title 8 §14300.35).

## Key information an LLM needs
**Information about the employee (fields 1–5):**
1. Full name
2. Street address
3. City, State, ZIP
4. Date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY)
5. Date hired (MM/DD/YYYY)
6. Sex: Male / Female (checkbox)

**Information about physician or other health care professional (fields 6–9):**
6. Name of physician or other HCP
7. If treatment was given away from the worksite: Facility name, Street, City, State, ZIP
8. Was employee treated in an emergency room? (Yes/No)
9. Was employee hospitalized overnight as an in-patient? (Yes/No)

**Information about the case (fields 10–18, right column):**
10. Case number from the Log (transfer from Form 300)
11. Date of injury or illness (MM/DD/YYYY)
12. Time employee began work (AM/PM)
13. Time of event (AM/PM or "Check if time cannot be determined")
14. What was the employee doing just before the incident occurred? Describe the activity and the tools, equipment, or material the employee was using. Be specific. (*Examples: "climbing a ladder while carrying roofing materials"; "spraying chlorine from hand sprayer"; "daily computer key-entry."*)
15. What happened? Tell us how the injury occurred. (*Examples: "When ladder slipped on wet floor, worker fell 20 feet"; "Worker was sprayed with chlorine when gasket broke during replacement"; "Worker developed soreness in wrist over time."*)
16. What was the injury or illness? Tell us the part of the body that was affected and how it was affected; be more specific than "hurt," "pain," or "sore." (*Examples: "strained back"; "chemical burn, hand"; "carpal tunnel syndrome."*)
17. What object or substance directly harmed the employee? (*Examples: "concrete floor"; "chlorine"; "radial arm saw." If this question does not apply to the incident, leave it blank.*)
18. If the employee died, when did death occur? Date of death.

**Completed by:** Signature, Title, Phone, Date (bottom left corner of form).

**Confidentiality notice:** Form contains employee health information; must be used to protect confidentiality per CCR Title 8 §14300.29(b)(6)-(10).

**PHI sensitivity:** This form contains employee name, DOB, hire date, medical treatment details, injury narrative. High PHI sensitivity — restrict access per CCR Title 8 §14300.35 and applicable HIPAA/state medical privacy laws.

## Open questions
- No revision date printed on form face — "Appendix C" designation and CCR Title 8 references are the primary identifiers. Check dir.ca.gov/dosh/etools/recordkeeping/ to confirm this is the current form.
- Some workers' comp first reports (Form 5021) or insurance forms contain sufficient equivalent information to substitute for Form 301 — this substitution is legally permitted and may be relevant when assessing an employer's recordkeeping compliance.
