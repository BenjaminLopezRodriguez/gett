## What it's for / when it's used
Used when an employee needs FMLA leave to care for a covered family member with a serious health condition. The employee's family member's physician completes this form. Covered family members under federal FMLA include: spouse, child (biological, adopted, foster, stepchild, legal ward, or child in loco parentis), or parent.

**Important FMLA vs. CFRA difference on family members:** Federal FMLA covers spouse, child, and parent. California CFRA covers a broader group: spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, parent-in-law, or "designated person" (anyone with a family-like relationship). For California workers, the CFRA certification (`cfra-certification-en`) may cover family members not covered by the federal WH-380-F.

## Key information an LLM needs (from published form descriptions — verify against actual form)
- Patient name and relationship to employee
- Date condition commenced, probable duration
- Description of medical facts (diagnosis optional — functional description of need for care is sufficient)
- Does patient require basic medical, hygiene, nutritional, or safety care or transportation? (Yes/No)
- Is the employee's presence necessary or would it be beneficial? (Yes/No, explanation)
- Will employee need intermittent leave or reduced schedule? Frequency and duration?
- HCP signature, license, specialty, address, phone
- Employee signature authorizing disclosure
