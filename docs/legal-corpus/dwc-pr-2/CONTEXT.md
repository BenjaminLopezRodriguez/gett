# DWC Form PR-2 — Primary Treating Physician's Progress Report

## What this file is
California ongoing progress report form filed by the primary treating physician (PTP) throughout a workers' compensation claim. Issued by California Division of Workers' Compensation (DWC). Form number: DWC Form PR-2, Rev. 10/2015. English. 2 pages. Filed at periodic intervals while the injured worker is under active treatment and has not reached maximum medical improvement (P&S status).

## What it's for / when it's used
Filed by the PTP to report treatment progress to the claims administrator and employer throughout an open workers' comp claim. Required as a periodic report every 45 days after the last report. Also filed whenever there is: a change in treatment plan, change in work status, need for referral/consultation, need for surgery or hospitalization, or response to a request for information.

**Do NOT use this form if the patient has been declared "Permanent and Stationary" (P&S) — i.e., has reached Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI).** Use DWC Forms PR-3 or PR-4 for P&S evaluations.

## Key information an LLM needs
**Reason for report (checkboxes at top — must check at least one):**
- Periodic Report (Required 45 days after last report)
- Change in treatment plan
- Release From Care
- Change in work status
- Need for referral or consultation
- Response to request for information
- Change in patient's condition
- Need for surgery or hospitalization
- Request for authorization
- Other

**Patient section:** Last name, first name, MI, street address, city, state, zip, sex, date of birth, occupation, phone number, date of injury.

**Claims Administrator section:** Name, claim number, address, employer name, phone, fax.

**Clinical sections:**
- Subjective Complaints (large free-text box)
- Objective findings: "Include significant physical examination, laboratory, imaging, or other diagnostic findings." (large free-text box)
- Diagnoses: up to 12 ICD-10 codes (same structure as Form 5021)
- Treatment Plan: "Include treatment rendered to date. List methods, frequency and duration of planned treatment(s). Specify consultation/referral, surgery, and hospitalization. Identify each physician and non-physician provider. Specify type, frequency and duration of physical medicine services (e.g., physical therapy, manipulation, acupuncture). Use of CPT codes is encouraged. Have there been any **changes** in treatment plan? If so, why?"

**Work Status (radio buttons — choose one):**
- Remain off-work until [date]
- Return to modified work on [date] with restrictions (specific: standing, sitting, bending, use of hands, etc.)
- Return to full duty on [date] with no limitations or restrictions

**Physician signature:** Original signature required (do not stamp). Must include: Cal. License Number, date of exam, specialty, physician address, phone. Signed under penalty of perjury per Labor Code §139.3.

**Privacy Notice** referenced at dir.ca.gov/od_pub/privacy.html.

**PHI sensitivity:** Contains patient name, DOB, address, occupation, diagnoses, treatment details, work restrictions. Standard PHI protections apply.
