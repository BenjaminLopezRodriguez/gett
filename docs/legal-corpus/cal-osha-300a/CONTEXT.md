# Cal/OSHA Form 300A — Annual Summary of Work-Related Injuries and Illnesses

## What this file is
California annual summary form required to be posted by all employers covered by CCR Title 8 §14300, summarizing the prior year's work-related injury and illness data from the Form 300 Log. Issued by California Division of Occupational Safety and Health (Cal/OSHA), Department of Industrial Relations. Form number: Cal/OSHA Form 300A, Rev. 7/2007, Appendix B. English. 1-page form.

## What it's for / when it's used
Required annual posting for all establishments covered by CCR Title 8 §14300, even if zero work-related injuries or illnesses occurred during the year. The employer compiles totals from the Form 300 Log and posts ONLY this Summary — not the Log itself — in a visible location from February 1 through April 30 of the following year.

The Log must be reviewed to verify completeness before completing this Summary. If no cases: write "0."

## Key information an LLM needs
**Number of Cases section:**
- (G) Total number of deaths
- (H) Total number of cases with days away from work
- (I) Total number of cases with job transfer or restriction
- (J) Total number of other recordable cases

**Number of Days section:**
- (K) Total days away from work
- (L) Total days of job transfer or restriction

**Injury and Illness Types section (totals from column M):**
- (1) Injuries
- (2) Skin disorders
- (3) Respiratory conditions
- (4) Poisonings
- (5) Hearing loss
- (6) All other illnesses

**Establishment information required:**
- Establishment name, street, city, state
- Industry description (e.g., "Manufacture of motor truck trailers")
- Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) code if known

**Employment information required:**
- Annual average number of employees
- Total hours worked by all employees last year (use optional Worksheet to estimate)

**Certification:** A company executive must sign. "Knowingly falsifying this document may result in a fine." Certifier must state: "I certify that I have examined this document and that to the best of my knowledge the entries are true, accurate, and complete."

**Posting requirement:** Post February 1 through April 30 of the year following the year covered by the form. This is a mandatory display — failure to post is a Cal/OSHA violation.

**Employee access:** Employees, former employees, and representatives have the right to review the Form 300 in its entirety and have limited access to the Form 301 (per CCR Title 8 §14300.35).
