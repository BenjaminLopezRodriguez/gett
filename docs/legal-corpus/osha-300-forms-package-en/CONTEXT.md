## What it's for / when it's used
Used by employers to track and report work-related injuries and illnesses as required by 29 CFR Part 1904.

**Who must keep these records:**
- Most employers with 11 or more employees throughout the previous calendar year
- Exemption: employers with 10 or fewer employees AND employers in certain low-hazard industries (see osha.gov/recordkeeping for exempt industry list)
- However, ALL employers regardless of size must report to OSHA any workplace fatality, in-patient hospitalization, amputation, or loss of an eye

**How to report immediately:**
- Call 1-800-321-OSHA (6742)
- Call local Area Office
- Online at osha.gov/pls/ser/serform.html

**Electronic submission:** Many employers must electronically submit OSHA form data — check osha.gov/injuryreporting/index.html.

**State Plan note:** Employers in State Plan states (including California, which has Cal/OSHA) should check with their State Plan — Cal/OSHA has its own equivalent forms (see `cal-osha-300`, `cal-osha-300a`, `cal-osha-301`). Federal OSHA forms may not apply directly in CA.

## Key information an LLM needs
**What is recordable:**
- Death
- Loss of consciousness
- Days away from work
- Restricted work activity or job transfer
- Medical treatment beyond first aid
- Cancer, chronic irreversible disease, fractured or cracked bone, punctured eardrum (per 29 CFR 1904.7)
- Needlestick/cut from contaminated sharp object
- Medical removal under OSHA health standard
- Tuberculosis infection
- Standard Threshold Shift in hearing

**What is NOT recordable (first aid only):**
Non-prescription medications, tetanus shots, wound cleaning/bandaging, eye patches, hot/cold therapy, non-rigid immobilization, drainage of blisters, finger/toenail drilling, eye irrigation, splinter removal, drinking fluids for heat stress, finger guards, massages

**Privacy concern cases** (do not use employee name on Form 300; use "privacy case" instead; keep separate confidential list):
- Injuries to intimate body parts or reproductive system
- Injury resulting from sexual assault
- Mental illness
- HIV infection, hepatitis, or tuberculosis
- Needlestick with infectious material
- Employee independently requests privacy

**Posting requirement:** Post Form 300A (Summary only, NOT the Log) February 1 through April 30 of the following year.

**Retention:** Keep Log and Summary 5 years following the year they pertain to.

**Illness classification categories (column M):**
1. Skin diseases/disorders
2. Respiratory conditions
3. Poisoning
4. Hearing loss
5. All other illnesses

**Day counting:** Count calendar days away from work or restricted activity, starting the day AFTER the incident. Stop counting at 180 days for either type.

## Open questions
- California employers subject to Cal/OSHA must use Cal/OSHA forms, not these federal forms. Confirm with client's employer which jurisdiction applies.
- Federal OSHA and Cal/OSHA requirements are substantively similar but have some differences; the Cal/OSHA forms reference CCR Title 8, not 29 CFR Part 1904.
