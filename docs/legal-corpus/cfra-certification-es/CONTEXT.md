# CFRA Certification of Health Care Provider (Spanish)

## What this file is
Spanish-language version of the California Family Rights Act (CFRA) medical certification form. Issued by California Civil Rights Department (CRD). Form number: CRD-E11P-SP, January 2023. Spanish. 2 pages. Direct translation of CRD-E11P-ENG; identical structure, same legal effect.

## What it's for / when it's used
Same purpose as the English version: provided by employer to Spanish-speaking employees who need CFRA leave and whose treating physician may prefer to complete the form in Spanish. The physician fills items 1–9; the employee fills item 10 separately. Legally equivalent to the English form under CFRA.

## Key information an LLM needs
**Title and heading:** "CERTIFICACIÓN DEL PROVEEDOR DE SERVICIOS MÉDICOS / Ley de Derechos de Familia de California (CFRA) o la Ley de Licencia Médica Familiar (FMLA)"

**CalGINA warning (top box, in Spanish):** "NOTA IMPORTANTE: La Ley de No Discriminación de Información Genética de California del 2011 (CalGINA) prohíbe a los empleadores... Para cumplir la Ley de No Discriminación por Información Genética (CalGINA), le pedimos que no dé ninguna información genética cuando responda a esta solicitud de información médica."

**Key confidentiality note on item 3:** "EL PROVEEDOR DE ATENCIÓN MÉDICA NO DEBE REVELAR EL DIAGNÓSTICO SUBYACENTE SIN EL CONSENTIMIENTO DEL PACIENTE" — HCP must NOT reveal the underlying diagnosis without the patient's consent. This is especially significant for workers' comp clients with sensitive diagnoses.

**Item 10 instruction (employee fills separately):** "NUMERAL 10 A SER COMPLETADO POR EL EMPLEADO QUE NECESITE LA LICENCIA FAMILIAR. A SER PROVISTO POR EL PROVEEDOR DE ATENCIÓN MÉDICA BAJO DIFERENTE COBERTURA." — Item 10 must be submitted under separate cover from the HCP.

**Form items (same structure as English):**
1. Employee name
2. Patient name (if different from employee); is patient a covered family member?
3. Date condition commenced (no diagnosis disclosure without consent)
4. Probable duration of condition
5. Can condition be described as a serious health condition? (Sí/No)
6. If for employee's own condition: Can employee perform any work? Essential functions?
7. If for family member: Does patient require basic care, hygiene, nutrition, safety, or transport? Is employee's presence justified?
8. Estimated time period care is needed
9. Intermittent leave / reduced schedule / time off for medical visits (frequency and duration)
10. Employee's statement about care to be provided (employee fills separately, under separate cover)

**Signatures required:** HCP (name printed, signature, date) + employee (signature, date).
